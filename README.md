# Data-Analysis-Ministry-Of-Health
## Project 1: Uncovering preventable clinical risk pathways in infant mortality (2020-2025)

This project analyzes paediatric clinical data from Machakos Level 5 Hospital to investigate the relationship between traditional gum scrubbing practices and infant mortality outcomes.

Using discharge and mortality records from 2020–2025, the analysis identifies a sharp rise in deaths, with mortality tripling over the six-year period. A key finding is the strong association between gum scrubbing and septic shock, with over 90% of related mortality cases involving septic complications. The study also highlights a critical risk window among infants aged 7–12 months and identifies seasonal mortality peaks, particularly in July.

Beyond clinical insights, the project uncovers significant data quality gaps, including underreported discharges and missing geographic data, which affect interpretation and decision-making.

The analysis provides actionable recommendations for:

Early sepsis intervention protocols
Targeted caregiver education
Improved clinical documentation practices
Strengthening data systems for public health planning

📊 Tools used: Python, pandas, data visualization
🏥 Context: Paediatric ward clinical data

## Project 2: Understanding the structure and gaps in mental health systems
This project maps the mental health landscape by analyzing stakeholder roles, service coverage, financing structures, and system-wide gaps across the sector.

The analysis reveals that mental health services are heavily concentrated in core areas such as access, advocacy, and community care, while critical domains like research, data systems, and surveillance remain underdeveloped. Stakeholder engagement is strong, with diverse participation across government, NGOs, and community organizations, though funding remains largely government-driven and limited in scope.

Key findings include:

High integration of mental health into general programs but gaps in specialized services (e.g., maternal mental health)
Uneven service coverage for vulnerable populations such as the elderly and persons with disabilities
Limited adoption of digital mental health solutions despite growing demand
Underutilization of data systems and surveillance tools for decision-making

The project highlights opportunities to:

Strengthen funding diversification
Expand digital and data-driven mental health solutions
Improve inclusivity in service delivery
Enhance workforce capacity through long-term training

📊 Tools used: Data analysis, stakeholder mapping, visualization
🌍 Focus: Health systems & policy insights

## Project 3: Depression among people living with HIV (PLHIV) in care at Machakos Level 5 Hospital (2023–2024)
This project analyzes clinical and psychosocial data from PLHIV attending Machakos Level 5 Hospital to investigate the prevalence, severity, and correlates of depression.

Using PHQ-9 scores and clinical records, the analysis finds that a notable proportion of patients experience moderate to severe depression, with certain socio-demographic and clinical factors strongly associated with higher risk. Key findings include the impact of occupation and ART regimen type on depression prevalence, as well as potential influences of CD4 count. The study also identifies gaps in behavioral and psychosocial reporting, such as inconsistent responses on social support and coping strategies.

The analysis provides actionable recommendations for:

Routine mental health screening for PLHIV
Targeted psychosocial support interventions
Enhanced clinical documentation of depression and psychosocial variables
Integration of mental health services into HIV care programs

📊 Tools used: Python, pandas, statsmodels, data visualization
🏥 Context: HIV outpatient clinic clinical and psychosocial data
